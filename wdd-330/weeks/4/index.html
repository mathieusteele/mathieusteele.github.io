The reading this week included an instroduction to working with forms and handling form events with JavaScript. This included intercepting and preventing form submission and handling focus and blur events. See Basic form events.

After covering the form basics, we went on to playing with other form controls beside the basic input as well as implementing event-based form validation. You can see the hero form to test it out. Later in the week I engaged in more form validation practice.

Our group meeting was very productive - we built an interactive tic-tac-toe board can you win? See what happens when you try to break it. Many edge cases are handled.

Later readings covered object-oriented programming and the modularization of javascript code and how to use 3rd-party code in projects. The concept of bundlers was introduced with webpack as an example. There are trade-offs with using libraries and care should be taken when evaluating them as part of a project solution. Why reinvent the wheel when the community has put in the work to solve low-level common problems? Many of these open-source libraries are battle-tested with a large community of users and tests to ensure that edge-cases are properly handled. There are also benefits to writing custom solutions by oneself, as you can individually test your use-case and are not dependent on the maintainer's decision of whether or not to support your use-case.
